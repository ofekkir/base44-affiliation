## Why Grammarly shows your word count (Oct 30, 2025) -- PAYWALLED
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/why-grammarly-shows-your-word-count)

Usage data emails create emotional commitment. Grammarly: "26.7M words analyzed." Customers cancel not because products fail but because they can't visualize success. Send one strategic email showing quantified success. Examples: ProfitWell ("$3,016 recovered"), Loom ("You avoided X meetings"), Snyder ("Hours saved"). Metrics = "social currency" -- shareable proof of success. GitHub contribution streaks leverage this too.

## Less availability = more virality (Jan 31, 2025) -- PAYWALLED
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/why-ultra-successful-products-disappear)

Time windows create virality. Examples: Wordle (one puzzle daily), BeReal (2-minute posting windows), Thursday (dating app limited to Thursdays). Two psychological triggers: (1) Scarcity Effect -- limited availability increases perceived value. (2) Shared Experiences -- synchronized access creates social moments.

## The genius way KitKat owns part of your day (Mar 29, 2024) -- PAYWALLED
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-kitkat-method-to-becoming-an)

"Moment Marketing" -- own a specific daily moment. KitKat's campaigns: billboards reminding people to take breaks, Google/YouTube campaigns targeting Gen-Z terms ("bad hair day," "I hate Mondays"), KitKat-shaped public benches. Recently linked "Have a Break" to ChatGPT (performs better after a "break").

## How to use mystery to win attention (Jan 26, 2024) -- PAYWALLED
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/random-surprises)

2016 study: discounts revealed at checkout (not upfront) generate greater appreciation. Contrast negative approach (LinkedIn profile viewers, Bumble pixelated faces) vs positive approach (Spotify shuffle, Netflix "surprise me"). Key: "Surprises ignite curiosity" via curiosity gap.
