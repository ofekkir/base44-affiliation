## Wikipedia's $169M "Maybe Later" (Jan 10, 2025) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/wikipedias-169-million-maybe-later)

Most "Maybe Later" buttons just close popups. Wikipedia instead requests an email address to send reminders later, contributing to raising $169M. Relies on cognitive consistency bias:
1. Clicking "Maybe Later" signals mental commitment
2. Providing email feels natural since user showed interest
3. Subsequent reminders trigger higher conversion

**Implementation**: Add email field after "Maybe Later" clicks on homepage popups, trial-ending notices, upgrade prompts, exit-intent popups. Reminder sequence: 24 hours, one week (highlighting missed benefits), final nudge with time-limited incentive.

## Color psychology is mostly nonsense (Feb 21, 2025) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/how-to-pick-the-perfect-color-in)

Challenges "blue = trust, green = growth" myth. References ecological valence theory. Three universal properties that work:
1. **Temperature (Warm vs Cool)**: Warm colors (red, orange) feel physically closer = urgency. Cool colors (blue, green) feel distant = calmness. Red "Buy Now" > blue consistently.
2. **Saturation (Vividness)**: Higher saturation = objects appear larger.
3. **Value (Lightness)**: Light backgrounds = trust/transparency. Dark = privacy/intimacy.

## Absurd pricing plans (Dec 22, 2023) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-new-trend-of-absurd-pricing-plans)

Examples: Tinder Select ($499/mo), Uber helicopter ($3,000), NUGGS ($10,000 for 200kg nuggets). Why it works:
1. **PR Goldmine** -- shock value creates headlines
2. **Decoy Effect** -- high anchor makes mid-tier appear cheap
3. **Brand Perception** -- premium tiers elevate overall brand
4. **Status Symbol** -- buyers flex absurd purchases
5. **Long Tail** -- HNW individuals and corporate expense accounts actually pay

Recommendation: Add a $999 tier alongside $9-$29 monthly options.

## 2 letters that make people click -- Identity Marketing (Jul 26, 2024) -- PAYWALLED
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/2-letters-that-make-people-click)

Add "-er" to convert actions to identities. Don't ask people to "help" -- ask them to be a "helper." Studies: "Helper" framing increased cleanup behavior by 50% in preschoolers. "Voter" framing increased turnout by 15%. Transform CTAs: "Help us improve" -> "Be a beta tester." "Upgrade your plan" -> "Become a pro user." "Learn more" -> "Be an early adopter."
