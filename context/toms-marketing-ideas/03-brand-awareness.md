## What to do if your marketing sucks (Oct 16, 2025) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/what-to-do-if-your-marketing-sucks)

Case study: Anthropic's transformation in 90 days via 3 moves:
1. **Full Opposite Direction** -- positioned Claude as "thinking partner" against AI "slop" instead of echoing industry messaging
2. **Made It Physical** -- weekend popup coffee shop with free coffee and branded "thinking" hats; subtle branding
3. **Celebrated the Person, Not the Product** -- messaging centered on user identity ("YOU think. We help")

14-step Anti-Positioning Playbook: identify competitor messaging patterns, discover audience fatigue, find overlap with product strengths, pick an opposite stance, create physical touchpoints, build identity-based messaging. "Safe is invisible." Also cites DuckDuckGo, Notion, Oatly.

## What 3 years at Wiz taught me about B2B marketing (Sep 18, 2025) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/what-3-years-at-wiz-taught-me-about)

Wiz reached $1B valuation in 18 months. Key tactics:
- **"Drops" over content calendars** -- surprise launches including children's book about cloud security, card games, specialized job board
- **Pattern Interruption Formula**: "expected format + unexpected audience" -- Broadway musicals for CISOs, Kubernetes training by golden retrievers, toy store for security pros
- **Visual differentiation**: bright colors and humor vs dark fear-based security aesthetics
- **Customer certificates** with names larger than company branding = 300% increase in social shares
- **Employee advocacy**: pre-created personalized graphics, pre-written posts ready for copy-paste
- **Frequency over perfection**: embrace 90% failure rate; failures disappear, successes compound

## The only thing better than word of mouth (Jun 27, 2025) -- FREE
[Original](https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-only-thing-better-than-word-of)

"Word of Sight" -- products designed to be visibly used, creating passive promotion without active recommendations:
- **Shazam**: minimal interface with prominent logo creates brand visibility when users hold phones up at concerts
- **Readwise**: @readwise tagging on Twitter turns private bookmarking into public advertising
- **Slack**: distinctive "ugly purple" for shoulder-surfing recognizability
- **Grammarly**: visible green dot during primary actions

Principles: public usage = bold colors, large logos. Video calls/screenshots = position branding in shareable areas. Core feature integration = make functionality inherently public.
