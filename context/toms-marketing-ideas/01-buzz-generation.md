## Viral Mechanics & Growth Loops

### The KitKat heist playbook (Apr 6, 2026) PAID
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/i-found-the-viral-chocolate-thief

- KitKat's stolen truck story went viral because they "gave everyone a job to do":
  - KitKat = victim (serious tone)
  - Other brands = suspects (simple format anyone could copy)
  - Audience = detectives (sharing, tagging)
- **Key**: Give your audience a "job" in the narrative

### The "URL Hunt" is my new favorite marketing game (Mar 23, 2026) FREE
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-url-hunt-is-my-new-favorite-marketing

5 tactics in one post:
1. **URL Hunt** -- buy 30+ domains with variations, only one reveals the winner. Cost $10-30 each, attendees spent 15+ min trying
2. **Counter-Conference Strategy** -- matcha bars, "No-AI Zones" outside competitor events
3. **Conversion Messaging** -- connect savings to tangible goods ("save enough for X")
4. **Screenshot-Based Advertising** -- internal Slack/chat screenshots on billboards
5. **Gender Respect at Events** -- feature women with speaking roles, not booth greeters

### Sneakiest growth play -- Moltbook (Feb 2, 2026) PAID
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-secret-growth-loop-inside-moltbook

- Moltbook got 200K tweets in 48 hours by requiring a public tweet as account verification step
- Readwise similar: saving tweets requires tagging @readwise
- **Principle**: Embed sharing into product mechanics, not as optional extra

### The world's #1 growth hack is back (Jan 26, 2026) PAID
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-worlds-1-growth-hack-is-back

- **Tactic**: Team-Based Voting Competition (Dropbox Space Race 2012 playbook)
- Lovable uses university voting leaderboard
- "Vote so WE ALL win" is more effective than individual rewards

### The 13 Viral Marketing Laws (Aug 21, 2025) FREE
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/the-13-viral-marketing-laws

1. **Thompson's Law** -- content spreads via huge accounts, not organic grassroots
2. **Purple Cow** -- differentiation or death
3. **Golden Hour** -- first 60 minutes determine viral trajectory
4. **Anti-Polish** -- authentic > polished
5. **Shitty Clickthroughs** -- all tactics decay over time
6. **Gladwell's Law** -- you need Connectors, Mavens, and Salespeople
7. **Me-First** -- frame as "now you can" not "we built"
8. **Expertise Curse** -- test copy with your mom
9. **Matthew's Advantage** -- look successful to become successful
10. **90-9-1 Rule** -- 90% lurk, 9% comment, 1% create
11. **Shareability Spectrum** -- design for the share moment
12. **Post-Purchase Rationalization** -- people justify decisions after the fact
13. **Reactance Theory** -- "don't click this" makes people click

### Transform boring content to viral (Jan 12, 2024) FREE
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/turn-boring-content-into-viral-hits

5 secrets from 250+ viral campaigns:
1. **Humor** -- shared truths everyone recognizes
2. **Surprise** -- modify one expected aspect
3. **Participation** -- make UGC easy
4. **Status** -- make audience feel like insiders
5. **Existing Resources** -- repurpose what you have

## Outreach & Brute Force

### How I caught and punished my Substack impersonator (Feb 5, 2026) FREE
https://www.marketingideas.com/p/how-i-caught-and-punished-my-substack

- **"Brute Force Marketing"** playbook:
  1. Identify someone with a problem your solution solves
  2. Use your product to fix it without asking
  3. Share finished work only, no pitching
  4. Make results specific and measurable
  5. Post publicly if no response
- Examples: Vanta sent Segment a compliance spreadsheet, Lychee created podcast clips for creators
